The Challenge

Nipah virus represents a significant biosecurity threat with mortality rates of 40-75%. Currently, there are no approved vaccines or treatments. Nanobodies - single-domain antibodies derived from camelids - offer a promising therapeutic approach due to their small size, stability, and ability to target cryptic epitopes.

This project explores whether large language models can assist in designing novel nanobodies that bind effectively to the Nipah virus receptor binding protein. We evaluated multiple frontier LLMs using the ipSAE (interface pseudo-Solvent Accessible Energy) metric to assess binding quality.
